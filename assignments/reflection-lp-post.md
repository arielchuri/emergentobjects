# Reflection Learning Portfolio Post

**Week:** Assigned Week 12 (2 week assignment), Due Week 14
**Due:** May 11
**Weight:** Part of Learning Portfolio engagement (10%)

## Learning Outcomes

This assignment maps to the following learning outcomes:

- **LO 6 - Life:** Reflect on personal responsibility and self-understanding through the creation of objects that respond to a utopian or dystopian future.
- **LO 7 - Reflection:** Document their process and reflect on their learning via the Parsons Learning Portfolio.

## Description

Write a comprehensive reflection on your learning journey throughout the semester, examining your growth in technical skills, creative thinking, and understanding of design as a tool for exploring possible futures.

## Requirements

- Write a reflective essay (600-800 words) that addresses:
  - Your development as a maker and designer this semester
  - Key moments of learning or breakthrough
  - Challenges you overcame
  - How your thinking about technology and futures evolved
  - Connections between this course and other aspects of your education
  - What you're most proud of
  - What you want to continue developing
- Include visual documentation showing your progression
- Connect to course learning outcomes
- Be honest and thoughtful

## Deliverables

- Complete Learning Portfolio post with:
  - Reflective essay (600-800 words)
  - Visual documentation of your semester journey
  - References to specific projects and moments
  - Future goals and intentions

### Reflection Prompts to Address

Your reflection should explore:

1. **Your Development:** How have you grown as a maker, designer, and critical thinker this semester?

2. **Key Moments:** What were the breakthrough moments, turning points, or significant learning experiences?

3. **Challenges & Growth:** What challenges did you face, and what did overcoming (or struggling with) them teach you?

4. **Evolving Thinking:** How has your understanding of technology, futures, design, and making changed?

5. **Connections:** How does this course connect to your other classes, your major, or your broader education?

6. **Pride Points:** What are you most proud of accomplishing or learning?

7. **Looking Forward:** What do you want to continue developing? What questions remain?

### What Makes Excellent Reflection
**Strong reflections demonstrate:**
- Specific examples and details (not generalities)
- Honest assessment of successes AND struggles
- Connection between making and thinking
- Recognition of personal growth
- Articulation of learning process
- Links to bigger ideas and contexts
- Clear, engaging writing
- Authentic voice

**Weak reflections show:**
- Generic observations without specifics
- Only listing what you did (not what you learned)
- Superficial treatment of challenges
- No personal voice or insight
- Missing connections to course goals
- Poor writing quality

### Assessment Focus
This assignment emphasizes:
- **Metacognition:** Thinking about your own learning
- **Integration:** Connecting experiences into understanding
- **Growth mindset:** Learning from the entire process
- **Communication:** Articulating learning clearly
- **Self-awareness:** Understanding your own development

### Why This Matters
Reflection is not just looking back - it's:
- Consolidating learning
- Recognizing growth you might not have noticed
- Articulating your development for future applications
- Practicing professional self-assessment
- Building portfolio narrative skills
- Preparing for continued learning

### Writing Tips
- Write honestly - this isn't about saying what you think we want to hear
- Use specific examples from your work
- Don't just describe - analyze and interpret
- Connect personal experience to bigger ideas
- Edit and revise - this is formal writing
- Read it aloud to check flow
- Have someone else read it for clarity
