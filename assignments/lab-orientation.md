# Lab Orientation

**Week:** 1 (Asynchronous)
**Due:** Week 2 (Feb 2)
**Weight:** Participation (part of 30%)

## Learning Outcomes

This assignment maps to the following learning outcomes:

- **LO 2 - Tools, Materials, Methods:** Write and debug basic Python code and create electronic circuits using microprocessors.

## Description

Complete the Making Center lab orientation to gain access to the tools, equipment, and workspaces you'll need throughout the semester. This is a required safety and access training.

## Requirements

- Schedule and attend a Making Center orientation session
- Complete all required safety training modules
- Familiarize yourself with:
  - Lab policies and hours
  - Tool checkout procedures
  - Safety protocols
  - Workspace etiquette
- Sign all required waivers and forms

## Deliverables

- Completed orientation certificate or confirmation
- Submit proof of completion to Canvas

## Grading

This assignment is **Pass/Fail** and counts toward participation (30% of final grade).

- [ ] Attend scheduled Making Center orientation session
- [ ] Complete all required safety training modules
- [ ] Sign all required waivers and forms
- [ ] Submit proof of completion to Canvas

### Why This Matters
Lab orientation is required because:
- **Safety:** You need to understand equipment safety protocols
- **Access:** You cannot use Making Center without completing orientation
- **Success:** Your final project requires Making Center resources
- **Community:** You need to understand shared workspace etiquette

### What to Expect
- Overview of Making Center facilities and locations
- Safety protocols for different equipment and areas
- Tool checkout and reservation procedures
- Workspace policies and etiquette guidelines
- Introduction to technicians and staff
- Required safety forms and waivers

### Tips
- Schedule early - orientation spots fill quickly at semester start
- Bring student ID and materials for note-taking
- Ask about specific equipment you're interested in using
- Get to know the technicians - they're invaluable resources
- Note the hours and policies for spaces you'll use most
