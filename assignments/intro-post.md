# Introduction Post

**Type:** Learning Portfolio Post
**Due:** Week 2 (Feb 2)
**Weight:** Participation/Complete-Incomplete

---

## Assignment

Post a brief introduction to the class Learning Portfolio discussion or Canvas. This is your chance to let us know who you are and start building our classroom community!

---

## Requirements

### 1. Photo
Include a photo of yourself. It can be:
- A selfie
- A recent photo
- An action shot doing something you love
- A creative self-portrait
- You with an object that's important to you

### 2. Basic Info
Share some (not all) of the following:
- Your name and pronouns
- Where you're from or where you're based now
- Your major or area of interest
- Why you're taking this course
- Previous experience with coding, electronics, or making (or total lack thereof!)
- A hobby, passion, or interest outside of school

### 3. The Improv Element

**Choose ONE of the following prompts:**

#### Option A: Object as Origin Story
Describe yourself through an everyday object that shaped who you are.

*Example: "I am a Nintendo Game Boy because I'm portable, pixelated, and require AA batteries (coffee) to function properly."*

#### Option B: Future Self Introduction
Introduce yourself as if you're posting from 10 years in the future. What are you working on? Where do you live? What problem are you trying to solve?

*Example: "Hi, I'm Jordan, posting from 2036 where I design atmospheric processors for underground cities. I'm taking this course to understand the vintage 2026 technology we now use in retro installations..."*

#### Option C: Two Truths and a Fabrication
Share three statements about yourself. Two are true, one is completely made up. Classmates can guess which is the fabrication in responses.

*Example:*
1. *I once performed in a flash mob at Grand Central Station*
2. *I can solve a Rubik's cube in under 30 seconds*
3. *I have a collection of 47 vintage calculators*

#### Option D: Your Personal Object
If you could create any electronic object for yourself (realistic or fantastical), what would it do and why would you need it?

*Example: "I'd create a 'Social Battery Indicator' that glows different colors based on my energy levels, so people know when I need quiet time versus when I'm ready to chat."*

---

## Format

Keep it brief: **100-200 words** total

**Structure:**
- Photo
- 2-3 sentences of basic info
- Your chosen improv prompt response

---

## Tips

- Be yourself! Authenticity > polish
- Have fun with the improv element
- Read and respond to at least 2 classmates' posts
- This is about building community, not being evaluated on writing skills
- If you're uncomfortable sharing a photo of your face, a creative alternative is fine

---

This course involves collaboration, critique, and creative risk-taking. Getting to know each other early helps build trust and makes the classroom a better place for experimentation and learning.
